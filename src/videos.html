Learn New Skills

Ceramics

Ceramic arts are created using clay, water, and a pottery wheel; the pieces are fired in a kiln, and then they are ready to be glazed and fired again. There are an endless number of objects you can make with ceramics, including tableware, chimineas, jewelry, candle holders, and flower pots.

Charcoal

Charcoal is made of willow branches heated at a high temperature. The charcoal can be willow or vine, and it can come in many forms, such as powered, compressed, or a pencil. Mistakes can be easily erased with a chamois or a paper towel.

Oil Paint

This type of paint is oil based, and another solvent should be used while painting. Opposed to other paint brushes, oil brushes are more stiff and durable. There is room to fix errors when it comes to oil painting due to the long time it takes for the paint to dry.

Acrylic Paint

Unlike oil paint, acrylic paint dries fast depending on how thin or thick you apply it. The paint can be applied to many surfaces, including fabrics, wood, and pottery.

Gesture Drawings

Gesture drawings are the study of the human figure in motion; the process of drawing the figure can be as quick as 10 seconds.
